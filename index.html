Trials and Tribulations of California's High-Speed Rail Project

This website is dedicated to the California High-Speed Rail Authority (CHSRA) and its contracting prowess.

The HSR25-89 Track and Systems Construction Contract (TSCC), valued at $3.5 billion, was announced in November 2025, eventually attracting two bidders:

  • Kiewit, Stacy Witbeck, Herzog ('Kiewit')
  • California High Speed Partners ('CHSP')

The proposal from Kiewit was analyzed and scored as it should have been. But CHSP were summarily disqualified instead. The reason? One member of CHCP's proposed personnel lacked a valid California Professional Engineer license! The fact that the individual was recognized internationally as a Chartered Engineer (CEng) was deemed irrelevant.

This deficiency could have easily been remedied by CHSP or waived by the Authority as being immaterial. The Authority refused to waive the requirement (even though it had issued three identical waivers for other key personnel roles one month earlier). Nor did CHSRA give CHCP sufficient time to rectify the situation (e.g., by hiring someone with the appropriate credentials). The protest filed by CHSP was dismissed one business day before the contract was awarded to Kiewit.

As a result of these shenanigans, the Board of Directors had to contend with having a "single bidder" for a $3.5 billion contract, which effectively became a single-source procurement. One can only wonder how much additional cost was thrown on the taxpayers' shoulders as a result of the Authority's abysmal "stewardship" of public assets.

Indeed, many Board members expressed profound unease about this - before unanimously awarding the contract to Kiewit anyway.
